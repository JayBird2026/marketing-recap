Marketing Director's Weekly Read
What the Numbers Are Telling Us — Week of April 6–12 (Revised)
  • $259,719 is the real number. Corrected revenue data adds $22,707 — primarily Website install revenue that was missing in the initial pull. This week was even stronger than first reported.
  • Website is now our #2 revenue channel at $39,316. With $40,207 in the sales pipeline behind it, this channel is a consistent revenue engine. The SEO investment is paying real dividends.
  • LSA confirmed at 17.26x ROAS. $75,194 in completed revenue at the highest ROAS we've tracked. This is non-negotiable budget to protect.
  • All three divisions contributed double-digit revenue. Install $183K, Service $38K, Plumbing $37K. This is the healthiest division balance we've had.
  • PPC HVAC is now in its third consecutive week below breakeven at 0.336x. This cannot continue without a structural change to targeting, creative, or landing page. A budget pause and audit is now warranted.
  • The avg ticket rose to $3,463. This validates premium positioning — higher-value jobs are closing, not just more jobs. Quality over volume is working.
  • 267 total abandoned calls — 148 untracked. The untracked number is now a pattern, not a one-week anomaly. At $3,463 per ticket, the weekly opportunity cost of this gap is approaching $500K. This needs an operational fix, not just a marketing note.
